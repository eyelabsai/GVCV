ICL — Implantable Collamer Lens

An overview by Gurpal Virdi, MD · San Francisco Bay Area

An ICL (Implantable Collamer Lens) is a phakic intraocular lens — a thin, biocompatible lens implanted between the iris and your natural lens to correct nearsightedness and astigmatism. Unlike LASIK, it doesn't remove or reshape corneal tissue, and it can be removed. The current generation is the EVO ICL.

I'm a refractive and cataract surgeon whose research and engineering focus on ICL sizing — matching the right lens to each individual eye.

ICL vs LASIK

Neither is universally "safer" — it depends on your eye. ICL is often preferred for higher prescriptions, thinner corneas, or dry-eye-prone eyes; LASIK is common for moderate prescriptions with very fast recovery. The right choice comes from a careful evaluation, not a default.

Who is a candidate?

Typically adults with myopia (and up to moderate astigmatism), especially high prescriptions or corneas not ideal for laser surgery. A full eye exam — including anatomy and retina checks — determines candidacy.

How an ICL is sized

Sizing is the central technical challenge: the same eye can vault very differently across lens sizes. Traditional sizing relies on single formulas and nomograms — the OCOS (STAAR) nomogram, the Reinstein formula, the KS formula (Kamiya–Shoji), the NK formula (Nakamura), the Parkhurst and Dougherty nomograms, and the Kane ICL formula. I co-founded and lead engineering for ICL Fit, built on the VAULT and VAULT-OCT ICL sizing models — machine-learning models for EVO ICL vault prediction, trained on 756 real surgical cases, that predict the best-fit size and post-op vault for each eye instead of relying on one fixed formula. I trained at Parkhurst NuVision under Dr. Greg Parkhurst (of the Parkhurst nomogram), one of the most-published EVO ICL teams in the U.S. For the science and my peer-reviewed research, see the EVO ICL page.

For patients: a full plain-language ICL guide — candidacy, recovery, risks, choosing a surgeon — is at ICLSurgery.com, which I co-review.
